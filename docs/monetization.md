# DadBuds Monetization Phases

This is an internal operating note. Keep phase 2 and phase 3 monetization copy out of the public launch surface until the product and partner proof are ready.

## Phase 1: Spokane founding group

- Do not discuss money publicly.
- The launch audience is mostly friends and acquaintances.
- The only public monetization inference is: "First 25 Spokane members get in free."
- Do not show donation buttons, pricing, subscription language, membership perks, or free trial copy.

## Phase 2: Post-25 trial

- After 25 members sign up, turn on a free month trial.
- Keep the free tier useful for browsing the calendar and coordinating plans.
- Do not paywall the core calendar before the local network is working.

## Phase 3: Local perks membership

DadBuds monetization should be a local perks membership, not a software subscription.

Potential Spokane perks:

- First beer free at partner trivia, capped by partner and cadence.
- Discounted Spokane Indians tickets through a group rate or promo code.
- Pickleball league entry included for members, with non-member drop-in priced separately.

Product rules:

- Perks attach to specific plans, not to the whole app.
- Non-members should still be able to browse the calendar.
- Membership unlocks at RSVP or check-in, not through generic feature gating.
- Avoid "premium RSVP" style positioning.

Operational requirements before selling the pass:

- Confirm 2-3 local partners.
- Define simple redemption: member list, QR, or manual check-in.
- Track partner headcount and redemption counts.
- Add product support only after the manual process works.

Likely later fields:

- `membership_status` on profile.
- Plan-level member perk badge.
- Optional promo code or redemption field at RSVP.
- Redemption log for partner reporting and abuse prevention.
